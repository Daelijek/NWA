N.W.A

Welcome to the world of N.W.A, a legendary hip-hop group that has left an indelible mark on musical and cultural history. Their bright and provocative lyrics, fearless atmosphere and bold ideas turned the idea of hip-hop and made a significant contribution to the development of the music industry.

About N.W.A

N.W.A (abbreviation for Niggaz With Attitudes) is an American hip hop group from Compton, California, which many consider one of the founders of the gangsta rap subgenre. This group existed from 1987 to 1991, and caused a lot of controversy due to the frank nature of their texts. Subsequently, the band was banned from many major US radio stations and even their tours were sometimes banned, but still the band has sold about 30 million copies of their CDs in the USA alone to date. Their first album, Straight Outta Compton, marked the beginning of a new era of gangsta rap, as their creativity and raising social issues were revolutionary within the genre.

INTERESTING FACTS

The band's famous debut album, released in 1988, became a catalyst for gangsta rap. In tracks like "F*** Tha Police", N.W.A expressed their position on social issues, causing excitement and questioning the status quo.

Release of the track "F*** Tha Police" led to serious controversies. The song aroused the indignation of the authorities, questioning police brutality and systemic racism. This moment singled out N.W.A as fighters for freedom of speech.

The breakup of N.W.A in the early 1990s did not stop the influence of the participants. Dr. Dre and Ice Cube achieved huge success in solo projects, rising to the top of the hip-hop scene. Their individual creative feats continue to inspire a new generation of artists.

"Efil4zaggin" (read as "N****z4Life") became the last studio album of the band. Released in 1991, it also became N.W.A's first and only album after Ice Cube left the lineup. Despite the difficulties, the album became a commercial success.

The legacy of N.W.A in the world of hip-hop and music is difficult to overestimate. The band not only opened new horizons in sound, but also became the voice of the generation, expressing its aspirations, struggles and experiences. Their influence continues to shape modern hip-hop and inspire artists in various genres.
